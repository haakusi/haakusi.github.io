Basis: a self-study week for connecting the W9-W14 concepts

This week introduces no new source chapter. Instead, Self-Study reorganizes eigenstructure, orthogonal approximation, and probability models. The goal is to select a mathematical representation from the form of the data and the question rather than memorize isolated formulas.

1. Connect Matrix Structure to the Data-Analysis Pipeline

  • Eigendecomposition describes invariant directions of a square matrix, while SVD describes dominant input and output directions of a rectangular matrix.
  • PCA finds high-variance axes using eigenvectors of a covariance matrix or the SVD of centered data.
  • Projection and least squares find the closest representation in a chosen subspace and separate the residual as an orthogonal component.

2. Use Problem Clues to Select a Tool

Problem cluePrimary toolCondition to verify
Dominant directions or repeated mapEigenvalues and diagonalizationSquare matrix and enough eigenvectors
Compression or rectangular dataSVD and low-rank approximationSingular-value sizes and rank
Excess observations or regressionProjection, QR, least squaresColumn space and residual orthogonality
Counts, measurements, dependenceProbability and joint distributionsIndependence, replacement, variable type

3. Practice Calculation, Conditions, and Interpretation Together

For each problem, write the objects and dimensions first, validity conditions second, and the meaning of the result last. A large singular value means an important data direction, a small residual means a good approximation within the model space, and a large positive covariance indicates linear co-movement.

Key Takeaways

  • Eigendecomposition, orthogonal projection, and SVD all separate data into important directions and remaining components.
  • Distribution choice depends on independence, sampling mechanism, and whether a variable is discrete or continuous, not on formula keywords.
  • Concept integration becomes stronger when the same result can be translated among algebraic calculation, geometric picture, and data-analysis meaning.

Practical Tips

  • Create a W9-W14 comparison sheet with one line each for definition, condition, core equation, AI example, and verification method.
  • Explain one centered data matrix through PCA, projection, SVD, and covariance to reveal where the views overlap.
  • Classify mistakes as arithmetic, missing condition, wrong distribution, or interpretation error, then solve another problem of that type.
