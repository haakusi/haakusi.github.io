An intensive course on artificial intelligence: agents, search, reasoning, symbolic AI, LLM agents, and the changing role of humans in AI-centered work.

W9 2026-05-05 · First class

Week 9

The first class introduced AI through intelligence, the Turing Test, Chinese Room, weak and strong AI, symbolic AI, LLM agents, memory, tools, MCP, and the importance of questions.
W10 2026-05-12 · Presentation

Week 10

Week 10 combined a book presentation, A* search implementation practice, and assignment feedback on BFS/DFS, goal test placement, failure cases, and complexity.
W11 2026-05-19 · Local search

Week 11

Week 11 practiced 100-bit string optimization with local search, random-restart hill climbing, random walk on a graph, and simulated annealing.
W12 2026-05-26 · Practice

Week 12

Week 12 was a Zombie Apocalypse practice day using evaluate-search.py and genetic-algorithm operators: selection, crossover, mutation, and repair.
W13 2026-05-25 - 05-31 · CSP

Week 13

Week 13 modeled problems with variables, domains, and constraints, then improved CSP solving through backtracking, MRV and LCV, forward checking, AC-3, and min-conflicts.
W14 2026-06-01 - 06-07 · Adversarial search

Week 14

Week 14 framed games as adversarial search, used minimax to reason about optimal replies, and applied alpha-beta pruning to preserve the result while examining fewer branches.
W15 2026-06-08 - 06-14 · Propositional logic

Week 15

Week 15 connected propositional syntax and semantics with models, entailment, sound and complete inference, truth-table checking, inference rules, CNF, and resolution.
W16 2026-06-15 - 06-21 · FOL & final assessment

Week 16

Week 16 expanded knowledge representation to first-order logic, quantifiers, instantiation, unification, and Generalized Modus Ponens, then concluded the course.
